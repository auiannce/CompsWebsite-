Project Overview

Our comps is a redesign of how algorithms are taught in CS classes. Buy focusing on the concepts of Greedy Algorithm and Divide and Conquer, we building a learning path that mixes technical practice with reflection on real-world impact.


We first looks at how algorithm courses often focus only on technical skill, but rarely talk about how algorithms shape people’s lives or how design choices can carry values. We chose to redesign the merge sort and greedy units because they are core topics that appear early in the CS path. They give a natural place for students to practice the usual goals while also stepping back to ask what happens when data is about people, and when “optimal” does not mean “fair.” By connecting technical ideas with real-world questions, our project tries to show that algorithm education should also teach responsibility, context, and care—not just efficiency.
