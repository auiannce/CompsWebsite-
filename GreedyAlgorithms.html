Greedy Algorithm

Overview

For the greedy part of our comps, we designed both in-class activities and a larger programming assignment. Together, they help students see how greedy choices work and why they are never fully neutral.

In many classes, greedy algorithms are taught as clean math: pick the best local choice, prove it works, and move on. What often gets lost is that “best” depends on how we measure cost and benefit, and those measurements come from real people and real values. Our in-class pieces show this step by step. Students practice the standard greedy ideas, but they also see how changing a weight or a rule can change who is helped or ignored.

The assignment extends this idea into a full system, where students encode costs and constraints that mirror real decisions. They discover that greedy paths depend on whose time, safety, or access we choose to prioritize.
